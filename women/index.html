AMDEP | Women and Media


Since 2002 Afghan women have had greater access to information through media, and new opportunities to train in media skills and to work in media organizations. Many of these gains have been made possible by United States government support for media development programs, including those implemented by Internews. However these gains are hard won, often unequally distributed, and often under threat.

The greatest opportunities have come to women living in Kabul and main towns in the north, central and western regions. In these areas women-managed media outlets have been established and greater acceptance of women having educational and work opportunities has allowed them to participate in media training and employment. While it is simplistic to divide the country into a more liberal north and west and more conservative south and east, those latter areas, and rural areas in general, usually provide fewer chances for women.

But wherever the opportunities have existed around the country, they have been enthusiastically and effectively used, and even in areas of few opportunities there are stories of women making brave choices and many sacrifices to play a role in the resurgent Afghan media.

AMDEP supports women-focused training and programmming, but wherever possible, seeks to include women within regular activities. Examples include support for national women's programming on Salam Watandar (produced by women-managed radio stations) and hosting of multimedia centers in Herat and Mazar within women-focused CSOs.

Women's civic engagement is also part of the AMDEP baseline studies conducted by Altai.
