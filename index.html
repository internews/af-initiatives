Internews, AMDEP and Afghanistan's Vibrant Media

Internews works in more than 40 countries supporting access to information through local media development, and citizen empowerment to use communication technology. Internews has worked in Afghanistan since 2002, implementing major media development programs, including the current Afghanistan Media Development and Empowement Project (AMDEP).

AMDEP started in late 2010, and is the third large US government media development project in Afghanistan since 2001. Internews is funded to implement AMDEP by the USAID Office of Democracy and Governance (DG). USAID/DG also funded the previous media development project from 2006-2010, while the initial US support for media development over 2002-2006 was via USAID's Office of Transition Initiatives.

This micro-site includes cumulative activity from all of those projects, but focuses on the latest AMDEP developments. The maps and information offer narrative and statistical entry points to the work done by Internews, its partners, and the many creative and resourceful Afghan journalists and citizens.

To view overall Afghan media usage figures please also explore the companion site 'Afghanistan Media Usage' that maps province-level data collected by Altai Consulting in 2010.
