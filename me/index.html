AMDEP | Monitoring and Evaluation


AMDEP has a major monitoring and evaluation (M&E) program, involving an in-house monitoring an evaluation unit in Kabul, independent research by Altai Consulting and additional M&E by project partners regarding their own activities.

This section focuses on the baseline research conducted by Altai Consulting. Altai has extensive experience in media M&E research, having completed extensive reports for USAID on the media sector in 2005 and 2010. Altai's 2010 research is the basis for the companion site 'Afghan Media Usage'.

    Location Comparisons

    The 12 locations provide a range of research contexts. Overall the location profiles provide a suitable representative sample of locations across Afghanistan. Individually they differ substantially in the amount of local media available, but also in population size, local economy, infrastructure, development projects, security and other relevant factors. Therefore direct comparisons between locations are not necessarily appropriate and the data in this section provides some of the background for differences between locations. Click images for larger versions. Note: the district-level data is from the Altai Consulting 2010 Media in Afghanistan research, and is not available for Narang or Cha Ab.
