AMDEP | Local Media


The growth of the broadcast media has been one of the great success stories of the last decade in Afghanistan.

When the Taliban government fell in 2001, Afghanistan's domestic broadcast media consisted of a moribund national broadcaster with bombed-out facilities. Ten years later there are several hundred local radio and television services serving as the backbone of a vibrant media sector. Internews has been instrumental in supporting the growth of the media sector in Afghanistan, through implementing USAID programs (since 2002) that have established local stations around the country.

Internews helps to establish local media organizations and infrastructure, to train media sector workers in a wide range of journalism, production, management and business skills, and to provide ongoing national programming, technical, policy and regulatory support. Internews' Afghanistan staff perform some of this work, but much is done by local partners.

The top map shows Internews established local radio stations (note: tv stations forthcoming in 2012) and training centers operated by Internews partner Nai. Hover on any location to see more details and link to coverage maps.

Play the video (below right) to see the coverage area of Internews-partner local radio stations grow over this period. New stations established each year appear briefly in red.

Most of these stations are also part of the Salam Watandar national radio network. Local stations broadcast Salam Watandar programming in addition to their locally-produced shows. The top map also shows the coverage areas for the stations broadasting Salam Watandar programming. Internews is developing a similar national supplementary television service -- Rasaa -- in partnership with Awanama. AMDEP partner Pajhwok Afghan News also provides content for many local media organizations.
